# Governance & Organizational Policies

**Sillionona (Private Organization) Governance Framework**

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the governance structure, decision-making processes, policies, and procedures for Sillionona, a private organization focused on technology innovation and sustainability. The governance framework ensures accountability, transparency, and effective management aligned with organizational values.

### Decision-Making Framework

#### Strategic Decisions (CEO Level)
- New product lines
- Major hiring/separations
- Large capital expenditures ($50K+)
- Partnership/investment agreements
- Organizational restructuring
- Policy creation

**Process:**
1. Proposal/analysis
2. Stakeholder input (24-48 hours)
3. Executive consideration
4. Final approval
5. Communication & implementation

#### Operational Decisions (Department Level)
- Project planning and execution
- Budget allocation within department
- Team scheduling and assignments
- Process improvements
- Technology selections
- Vendor management

**Process:**
1. Department lead assessment
2. Relevant stakeholder consultation
3. Decision + documentation
4. Team communication
5. Implementation

#### Tactical Decisions (Team Level)
- Day-to-day task execution
- Code implementation details
- Team coordination
- Immediate problem solving
- Process adjustments

**Process:**
1. Assess situation
2. Execute (communicating changes as needed)
3. Document decision rationale
4. Feedback and adjustment

### Decision Authority Matrix

| Decision Type | Authority | Consultation | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic vision | Founder | Leadership team | Quarterly |
| New products | Founder + Leadership | Team | Monthly |
| Major budget (>$50K) | Founder | Department heads | As needed |
| Hiring/Termination | Founder + Department head | HR | As needed |
| Technology selection | Department head | Team | 1-2 weeks |
| Project scope | Department head + Product | Team | 1 week |
| Daily operations | Department/Team lead | Relevant parties | Daily |
| Emergency decisions | On-duty lead | Relevant stakeholders | Immediate |

## Meeting Schedule & Communication

### Regular Meetings

#### Weekly
- **Engineering Standup** (Mondays)
  - Attendees: Backend, Frontend teams
  - Duration: 30-45 minutes
  - Focus: Progress, blockers, coordination

- **Design Review** (Wednesdays)
  - Attendees: Design, Frontend, Product
  - Duration: 1 hour
  - Focus: Design implementation, feedback

#### Bi-Weekly
- **All-Hands Meeting** (Every other Friday)
  - Attendees: Entire organization
  - Duration: 1 hour
  - Focus: Updates, challenges, celebrations

- **Leadership Sync** (Tuesdays)
  - Attendees: Department heads
  - Duration: 1 hour
  - Focus: Strategic alignment, cross-team issues

#### Monthly
- **Strategic Planning** (First Monday)
  - Attendees: Leadership team
  - Duration: 2 hours
  - Focus: Month ahead, priorities, risks

- **Financial Review** (Last Friday)
  - Attendees: Leadership
  - Duration: 1 hour
  - Focus: Budget, spend, forecasting

- **Customer/Community Feedback Session** (Mid-month)
  - Attendees: Product, Engineering, Design
  - Duration: 1 hour
  - Focus: User feedback, roadmap adjustment

#### Quarterly
- **Strategic Review** (Mid-quarter)
  - Attendees: Full organization
  - Duration: Full day
  - Focus: Direction, learnings, planning

- **Performance Review Cycle** (End of quarter)
  - Attendees: Managers + Individual employees
  - Duration: 1 hour per person
  - Focus: Goals, development, feedback

## Policy Framework

### Code of Conduct

#### Core Principles

1. **Respect & Inclusion**
   - Treat all with respect and dignity
   - Inclusive and welcoming environment
   - Zero tolerance for discrimination/harassment
   - Celebrate diversity

2. **Integrity & Honesty**
   - Honest and transparent communication
   - Own mistakes and learn
   - No dishonesty or fraud
   - Keep commitments

3. **Quality & Excellence**
   - Strive for high quality
   - Continuous improvement mindset
   - Pride in work
   - Excellence in delivery

4. **Accountability**
   - Own your responsibilities
   - Follow through on commitments
   - Transparent about challenges
   - Learn from failures

5. **Collaboration**
   - Work together effectively
   - Share knowledge freely
   - Support team members
   - Collaborative problem-solving

#### Expected Behaviors

**To Do:**
- Communicate respectfully
- Admit mistakes promptly
- Help teammates succeed
- Ask questions to understand
- Give and receive feedback constructively
- Maintain confidentiality appropriately
- Follow processes and guidelines
- Take initiative on improvements

**Not To Do:**
- Discriminate or bully
- Misrepresent facts
- Take unnecessary shortcuts
- Ignore teamwork
- Spread rumors or gossip
- Disclose confidential information
- Ignore compliance requirements
- Retaliate against feedback

### Confidentiality & NDA

#### Confidential Information

**What is Confidential:**
- Product designs and roadmaps
- Financial information
- Customer data and lists
- Strategic plans
- Trade secrets and technical innovations
- Personnel information
- Proprietary processes
- Source code (unpublished)

**What is NOT Confidential:**
- Public information
- General knowledge
- Publicly available code
- Regulatory disclosures
- Legally required disclosures

#### Obligations

**All Team Members Must:**
- Keep confidential information secure
- Share only on need-to-know basis
- Use secure communication
- Return materials upon departure
- Maintain confidentiality after employment
- Report breaches immediately

**Duration:**
- During employment + 3 years after
- Trade secrets: Indefinite
- Regulatory disclosures: As required

#### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

**Standard NDA Required For:**
- All employees
- Contractors and consultants
- Partners and vendors (case-by-case)
- Advisors and board members

**Enforcement:**
- Breach remedies include injunction
- Damages recovery pursued
- Legal action if necessary
- Termination for cause if breach

### Intellectual Property Policy

#### Ownership

**Company Owns (Work-Made-for-Hire):**
- Code written during work hours
- Designs created at work or using company resources
- Documentation and specifications
- Inventions from job responsibilities
- Updates and improvements to company IP

**Employee Retains:**
- Pre-existing IP from before employment
- Personal projects outside work time (disclosed)
- IP developed purely on personal time/equipment
- Learning and knowledge development

#### Assignment & Registration

**For Company-Owned IP:**
- Automatic assignment to Sillionona
- Formal IP assignment agreements required
- Patent ownership transferred to company
- Copyright assignments executed
- Trademark rights assigned

**Process:**
1. Create/develop work
2. Document invention/creation
3. File assignment (if required)
4. Register trademarks/patents (as appropriate)
5. Maintain records

#### Source Code Management

**Public/Open Source Code:**
- License clearly specified (MIT, Apache 2.0, etc.)
- License header on all files
- Repository README includes license
- Proper attribution provided
- No proprietary code mixed in

**Proprietary Code:**
- Marked as proprietary
- Access controlled
- Private repositories
- No public distribution
- Copyright notice included

### Conflict of Interest Policy

#### What Constitutes Conflict of Interest

**Personal Financial Interest:**
- Own significant interest in competitor
- Financial interest in vendor/partner
- Outside employment with competing company
- Receiving personal benefits from business decisions

**Family/Personal Relationships:**
- Direct report relationship with family
- Hiring decisions involving relatives
- Partner/spouse employment with competitor
- Close personal relationships affecting decisions

**Gifts & Entertainment:**
- Valuable gifts from vendors/partners
- Lavish entertainment/events
- Loans or financial arrangements
- Unusual privileges or benefits

#### Disclosure & Resolution

**Disclosure Required For:**
- Any actual conflict
- Potential conflicts
- Perceived conflicts (transparency)
- When becoming aware of potential

**Process:**
1. Disclose immediately to manager/leadership
2. Provide full details
3. Leadership assessment
4. Mitigation measures
   - Recusal from decision
   - Separation of duties
   - Monitoring/oversight
   - Disclosure to affected parties

**Consequences of Non-Disclosure:**
- Disciplinary action
- Termination for cause
- Legal action (if severe)

### Data Privacy & Protection

#### Personal Data Collection

**What We Collect:**
- User account information
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Device information
- Usage data
- Location data (if applicable)
- Payment information (if applicable)

**Lawful Basis:**
- Consent for non-essential data
- Contract performance
- Legal obligation
- Legitimate interests (transparency required)

#### Data Security

**Protections Required:**
- Encryption in transit (TLS/SSL)
- Encryption at rest (planned)
- Access controls
- Authentication requirements
- Regular backups
- Security monitoring
- Incident response procedures

**Data Protection Standards:**
- GDPR compliance (for EU data subjects)
- Privacy by design
- Data minimization
- Purpose limitation
- Retention limits

#### User Rights

**Rights Guaranteed:**
- Access their personal data
- Understand how data is used
- Correct inaccurate data
- Delete/erase data (right to be forgotten)
- Data portability
- Object to certain processing
- Not be subject to automated decisions

**Response Timeframe:** 30 days

#### Data Breach Response

**Upon Discovery:**
1. Assess severity and scope (immediately)
2. Contain the breach (within 24 hours)
3. Notify affected users (within 72 hours)
4. Report to authorities (if required)
5. Document details
6. Long-term remediation
7. Communication with stakeholders

### Security Policy

#### Information Security Requirements

**Access Controls:**
- Strong passwords (12+ chars, mixed case, symbols, numbers)
- Multi-factor authentication (where available)
- Principle of least privilege
- Role-based access control
- Regular access reviews

**Device Security:**
- Passwords/PINs on all devices
- Encryption on devices storing data
- Software updates current
- Antivirus software installed
- No public WiFi for sensitive work

**Communication Security:**
- Use official email only
- Encrypt sensitive communications
- No sensitive data via text/SMS
- Secure file sharing tools
- No sharing of passwords via email

**Physical Security:**
- Desk clean policy (no data left out)
- Lock screens when away
- Secure document disposal
- Badge access where applicable
- Visitor management

#### Security Incident Response

**Response Priority:**
1. Quick critical systems
2. Assess damage
3. Contain incident
4. Investigate root cause
5. Fix vulnerability
6. Communication
7. Prevention measures

**Incident Reporting:**
- Report immediately to leadership
- Document details
- Preserve evidence
- Follow investigation protocols
- No destruction of data

## Dispute Resolution

### Internal Dispute Resolution

**Level 1: Direct Discussion**
- Parties attempt resolution directly
- Documentation of discussion
- Timeframe: 5 business days

**Level 2: Manager/Department Head**
- Involve immediate manager
- Informal mediation
- Proposed resolution
- Timeframe: 10 business days

**Level 3: Executive Review**
- Escalate to leadership
- Formal review process
- Investigation (if needed)
- Documented resolution
- Appeal opportunity
- Timeframe: 20 business days

### External Dispute Resolution

**For Unresolved Disputes:**
- Mediation (neutral third party)
- Arbitration (if agreed)
- Litigation (last resort, as applicable)
- Governed by contract terms

**Cost Responsibility:**
- Organization covers reasonable costs
- Unless bad faith or misconduct
- Individual responsibility determined case-by-case
